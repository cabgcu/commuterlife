Chapter 1

Getting Started

Welcome to Commuter Life — a workspace built for teams to coordinate projects, shifts, equipment, and events from a single app. This manual walks you through every feature so you can hit the ground running.

Logging In

1
Open the Commuter Life app in your browser (or from your home screen if installed).
2
Enter the email address associated with your workspace account.
3
Returning user: Enter your password and tap Sign In.
4
New account: You'll be prompted to create a password and optionally upload a profile photo.
5
Once signed in, you land on the Board view — your main workspace.
Forgot your password? Tap "Forgot password?" on the login screen and check your email for a reset link.

Installing as a Home Screen App

Commuter Life works as a Progressive Web App (PWA), which means it can be installed directly to your phone or computer for a native-app feel — no App Store required.

  • iPhone/iPad: Open in Safari → tap the Share button → Add to Home Screen.
  • Android: Open in Chrome → tap the three-dot menu → Add to Home Screen.
  • Desktop (Chrome/Edge): Look for the install icon in the address bar and click it.

Chapter 3

Board View

The Board is your team's central workspace — a Kanban board with columns representing stages of work. Cards move left-to-right as work progresses.

Understanding the Board

Each column represents a workflow stage (e.g. To Do, In Progress, Done). Each card is a task. Drag cards between columns to update their status.

Creating a Task

1
Click the + Add Task button at the top or bottom of any column.
2
Type the task title and press Enter to create quickly — or click the card to open the full details panel.
3
Fill in optional details: description, due date, assignees, labels, checklist, and attachments.
4
Changes save automatically — no need to hit a Save button.

Task Details Panel

Click any card to open its full details. Here's what you can add:

📝 Description

A rich-text field for notes, context, or links. Supports formatted text and hyperlinks.

📅 Due Date

Pick a date from the date picker. Overdue cards are highlighted in red; tasks due within 48 hours show in orange.

👥 Assignees

Drag team member avatars onto the card, or click the assignee button inside the panel. Multiple people can be assigned to the same task.

🏷️ Labels

Color-coded tags for categorization: blue, purple, pink, green, orange, and lime. Click a label pill to toggle it on or off.

✅ Checklist

Break a task into sub-items. Each checklist item can have its own assignee. Tick items off as they're completed — the card shows a progress indicator.

📎 Attachments

Upload files or images directly to a card. Images show a thumbnail preview; other files show as download links.

💬 Comments & Activity

Leave comments for teammates. All changes (status moves, assignee changes, due date edits) are logged in the activity feed.

🖼️ Cover Image

Add a photo to the top of a card to make it visually distinct. Inside the card panel, click Upload image to set a cover. To remove it, click Remove Cover. Cover images appear as a banner on the card in the Board view.

Checklist Templates

If your team uses the same checklist steps repeatedly, save them as a reusable template so you don't have to retype them every time.

Saving a template

Open a card that has a checklist you want to reuse. In the checklist section, click Save as Template, give it a name, and confirm. The template is saved to the workspace and available to everyone.

Loading a template

Open any card's checklist section and click Load Template. Pick a template from the list — its items are added to the current checklist instantly. Duplicate items are skipped automatically.

Deleting a template

Open the Load Template dialog and choose Delete a template… at the bottom of the list. Select which template to remove and confirm.

Searching Cards

Use the 🔍 Search bar in the top controls to filter cards in real time. As you type, the Board narrows down to only matching cards. Search is case-insensitive and matches card titles and descriptions. Clear the search field to restore the full board.

Moving & Organizing Tasks

  • Drag & drop a card to a different column to change its status.
  • Use the status dropdown inside the card panel to change the column without dragging.
  • Long-press on mobile to enter drag mode.

Bulk Actions

Need to move or delete several cards at once? Tap the bulk-select toggle on the Board to enter multi-select mode. Each card gets a checkbox.

  • Select cards by clicking their checkboxes. A bar appears at the bottom showing how many are selected.
  • Move all selected to any column using the column dropdown in the bar, then click Move.
  • Delete all selected by clicking the red Delete button. You'll be asked to confirm.
  • Click ✕ Clear in the bar to deselect everything and exit bulk mode.
Cards auto-save every time you make a change. If you close the panel mid-edit, your work is already saved.
Chapter 4

Automations

Automations are rules that run in the background without you lifting a finger. Each rule watches for a specific event (the trigger) and then performs one or more actions automatically. Once set up, they save your team time on repetitive steps — like adding labels, moving cards, or assigning members.

Open Automations by clicking the ✨ Automations button in the top controls bar. The button header also shows a live count of how many times your rules have fired in total.

Creating a New Rule

1
Click ✨ Automations in the top bar to open the Automations panel.
2
Click + Create Rule. The rule builder expands below.
3
Under ⚡ When this happens, pick a trigger from the dropdown (see full list below).
4
If the trigger needs a value (e.g., a column name, a label name, or a number of days), fill it in.
5
Under 👉 Then do all of this, click + Add Action and choose what should happen.
6
Configure each action's parameters. Add as many actions as you need — they all run together.
7
Click Save Rule. The rule appears in your list immediately and starts running.

Available Triggers

Choose one trigger per rule. Some triggers require an extra value to be set.

Example Rules

Auto-assign a member when a card is created

Trigger: Card created  →  Action: Assign member → "Jane Doe"
Every new card automatically lands with Jane as the assignee.

Add a checklist when a card moves to "In Progress"

Trigger: Card moved → "In Progress" column  →  Action: Add checklist items → "Research, Draft, Review, Publish"
Cards in progress always start with the same standard checklist.

Flag urgent cards 3 days before due

Trigger: Due soon → 3 days  →  Actions: Set priority → High & Add label → "Urgent" (Red)
Upcoming deadlines are automatically flagged so nothing slips.

Move cards to Done when checklist is complete

Trigger: Checklist completed  →  Action: Move to list → "Done"
When every checklist item is ticked, the card moves itself.

Managing Your Rules

All saved rules appear as cards in the Automations panel. Each card shows the trigger, the actions, how many times it has run, and when it last fired.

Enable / Disable a rule

Use the toggle switch on the right side of the rule card to pause or resume a rule without deleting it. A disabled rule stays in your list but won't fire.

Delete a rule

Click the × button on the rule card. You'll be asked to confirm before the rule is permanently removed.

Edit a rule

Rules can't be edited in place. To change a rule, delete it and create a new one with the updated settings.

Automations run on the active workspace only. If you switch workspaces, each workspace has its own independent set of rules.
Chapter 5

Calendar View

The Calendar view shows all tasks with due dates laid out on a monthly grid — great for spotting deadline clusters and planning workload distribution.

Reading the Calendar

  • Each task pill on a day represents one task with a due date on that day.
  • Today's date is highlighted with a blue ring.
  • Hover over a task pill to see a quick summary without opening the full panel.
  • Click a task pill to open its full details.

Rescheduling Tasks

Drag a task pill from one day to another to update its due date instantly. No need to open the card manually.

If the current month looks crowded, switch to the Board view and filter by label to focus on a specific category of work.
Chapter 6

Due Soon

Due Soon is a filtered, time-sorted list of tasks that need attention. It's the fastest way to triage what's urgent without scrolling through the entire board.

Understanding the List

  • Tasks are sorted from most urgent (overdue or due today) to furthest out.
  • A countdown shows how many days or hours remain for each task.
  • Priority dots indicate task urgency: High Medium Low

Click any item to open its full card details, just like on the Board.

Chapter 7

Storage View

Storage helps your team track physical equipment and supplies organized into labeled bins. Anyone can check items out or back in, and the system keeps a history.

Browsing Bins

The Storage view shows a grid of all bins. Each bin card displays its name, emoji icon, and a status indicator showing how many items are currently checked out.

Viewing a Bin's Contents

1
Click any bin card to open its detail panel.
2
You'll see a list of all items inside the bin along with their quantities and current status (Available or Checked Out).
3
A checkout history log shows who checked items out and when they were returned.

Checking Out an Item

1
Open the bin containing the item.
2
Click the item row to select it.
3
Tap Check Out. The item status changes to Checked Out and is logged with your name and timestamp.

Returning an Item

Open the same item and tap Check In. The item returns to Available status.

QR Code Access

Each bin has a QR code (visible inside the bin panel). Scanning it opens that bin's public page — useful for physical labels on the bin itself. Anyone with the link can view contents and check items in/out without logging in.

Items marked as checked out will show in orange on the bin grid. Make sure to check items back in after use so teammates know what's available.
Chapter 8

Schedule View

The Schedule view coordinates who is working when. It shows assigned shifts, open volunteer slots, and worker availability — all in one place.

Reading the Schedule

On desktop, you'll see a 7-day week grid. Each column is a day; shift cards inside each column show the worker's name, role, and start/end time.

On mobile, toggle between a Week timeline and a Month calendar using the mode switch at the top of the view.

Setting Your Availability

Workers tell the team when they're free to work by filling out their availability. Admins use this information when scheduling shifts.

1
Ask your admin to send you your personal availability link (they generate it from your worker profile).
2
Open the link in your browser. You'll see a form with Monday through Sunday.
3
Check the Available box for each day you can work. Time fields appear — set your available From and To times (defaults to 9 AM – 5 PM).
4
Click Submit Availability. A green confirmation banner confirms it was saved.

Your availability appears in the availability cards below the shift grid, visible to your admin when they're building the schedule.

Adding a Shift Admin

1
Click the + Add Shift button inside a day column.
2
Select the worker from the dropdown — or choose 🟢 Open — workers can sign up to post a volunteer slot (see below).
3
Choose a role and set the start and end times.
4
Save. The shift appears on the grid and the assigned worker receives a notification.

Open / Volunteer Shifts

Admins can post shifts that any team member can claim. These show up on the schedule as open slots highlighted for everyone to see.

Posting an open shift Admin

When adding a shift, select 🟢 Open — workers can sign up as the worker. Save the shift. All team members immediately receive a push notification: "A volunteer shift is available on [date] at [time]."

Claiming an open shift

Open shifts show a Claim button on the shift card. Tap it — the shift is immediately assigned to you, your name replaces "Open" on the grid, and you start receiving shift reminders.

Unclaiming a shift

Changed your mind? Tap the shift you claimed and choose Unclaim. The shift returns to open status so a teammate can pick it up.

Requesting a Shift Swap

If you can't make a shift you're scheduled for, you can request that a teammate cover it. Both sides are notified and the swap doesn't take effect until it's approved.

1
Find your shift on the schedule and click Request Swap (or access it through the shift's menu).
2
In the Request Shift Swap modal, select the shift you want to swap from the My Shift dropdown. If it's a recurring shift, enter any date from that week.
3
Choose a teammate from the Covered by dropdown — this is the person you're asking to take your shift.
4
Optionally add a note (e.g., "I have a doctor's appointment") so your teammate has context.
5
Click Submit. The teammate receives a 🔄 Swap Request notification immediately.

Responding to a Swap Request

When someone requests a swap with you, you'll receive a push notification. Swap requests appear in the Swaps tab of the Schedule view.

Approving a swap

Click Approve on the request. The shift is instantly reassigned to you on the schedule. You'll receive a 📅 Shift Assigned notification, and the requester gets a ✅ Swap Approved notification.

Denying a swap

Click Deny. The shift stays with the original worker, who receives a ❌ Swap Denied notification. No changes are made to the schedule.

Admins can see all pending, approved, and denied swap requests in the Swaps tab to keep an eye on coverage.

Shift Reminders

Workers automatically receive push notifications:

  • 24 hours before their shift starts.
  • 1 hour before their shift starts.

Make sure push notifications are enabled in your browser (see the Notifications section).

Sharing the Availability Page

Admins can share a public availability link so teammates can view the team's schedule without logging in — handy for posting in a group chat or pinning to a bulletin board.

Chapter 9

Manager View Admin

Manager is an admin-focused hub for planning events, tracking budgets, and storing event photos. It's organized into four sub-tabs.

This view is fully visible to all users on desktop. On mobile, it may be restricted to admin accounts.

Events Tab

Track every event your team is running. Each event has a name, date, assigned workers, and a status badge:

  • OK — On track, no issues.
  • Warn — Needs attention.
  • Action Needed — Urgent issue to resolve.

To add a new event: Click + New Event, fill in the event name, date, and assigned team members, then save.

Toggle between List view and Card view using the view switcher in the top-right of the tab.

Calendar Tab

A 7-column calendar grid plots events by date. Click an event pill to see its details. Great for spotting scheduling conflicts at a glance.

Budget Tab

Track expenses for each event. Add a new expense with a category, amount, and description. All expenses are displayed in a table sorted by event and category.

Photos Tab

Upload and manage photos from events. Photos display in a grid gallery. Click any photo to view it full size; admins can delete photos from this view.

Chapter 10

Settings

Settings is where you manage your profile, control workspace preferences, and handle team membership.

Your Profile

  • Profile Photo: Click your avatar to upload a new photo. Supported formats: JPG, PNG, WebP.
  • Display Name: Your full name as it appears across the app.
  • Email: The email address used to log in.

Workspace Settings Admin

Admins can customize the workspace to match your organization's branding:

  • Workspace Name — The title shown at the top of the app.
  • Logo — Upload a logo image that appears in the header.
  • App Icon — The icon displayed when the app is installed on a device.
  • Board Background — Set a background image or color for the Board view.

Workspaces Admin

Commuter Life supports multiple workspaces — for example, one per academic year or per team. Each workspace has its own completely separate board, schedule, storage, and members. Click the workspace name in the header (or go to Settings) to open the Workspace Switcher.

Switching workspaces

In the Workspace Switcher modal, click any workspace name in the list to switch to it instantly.

Creating a new workspace

Click + New Workspace in the modal. Enter a name (e.g., "2025–2026") and confirm. The new workspace starts empty — you'll need to add team members and set it up from scratch.

Renaming or deleting a workspace

Hover over a workspace in the list to reveal Rename and Delete buttons. Deleting a workspace is permanent and removes all its data.

Setting a default workspace

In Settings, use the Default Workspace dropdown to choose which workspace loads automatically when anyone opens the app.

Managing Team Members Admin

1
Scroll to the Team Members section in Settings.
2
To add someone, enter their email address and click Invite. They'll receive an email to set up their account.
3
To remove someone, find their name in the list and click the remove (×) button next to their name.
Removing a team member revokes their access immediately. Any tasks assigned to them will remain on the board.

Importing Tasks from Monday.com Admin

If your team previously used Monday.com, you can pull existing tasks directly into the Board. Find the Monday.com Integration section in Settings.

1
Enter your Monday.com API Token and the Board ID you want to import from.
2
Map the Monday.com column fields (event name, date, description, etc.) to the corresponding fields in Commuter Life.
3
Click Save Monday.com Settings. The import runs automatically and creates cards on your board.
4
Already-imported items won't be duplicated on subsequent saves — the app tracks which items have been pulled in.
To re-import items that were previously skipped, use the Refresh Old Imports button to clear the import history.

Deleting a Workspace Irreversible

Admins can permanently delete the entire workspace including all tasks, schedules, and storage data. This action cannot be undone. You'll be asked to confirm by typing the workspace name before deletion proceeds.

Notifications

Commuter Life sends notifications both inside the app (in-app) and as push notifications to your device, so you never miss an important update.

In-App Notifications

Tap the bell icon (top-right on desktop, bottom nav on mobile) to open your notification panel. Each notification is listed with a timestamp. Unread notifications are highlighted; tap one to mark it as read and jump to the related item.

Push Notifications

Push notifications appear on your device even when the app isn't open. When you first use the app, your browser will ask permission to send notifications — click Allow to enable them.

Chapter 12

Tips & Shortcuts

Power User Tips

  • Drag tasks on the calendar to reschedule them — faster than opening the card.
  • Assign multiple people to a single task for shared ownership.
  • Use labels consistently across your team so filters and visual scans work well (e.g., always tag client work "blue", internal work "green").
  • Checklist items can have separate assignees — break a task into clear sub-owners so nothing falls through the cracks.
  • Save checklist templates for recurring project types so new cards start with the right steps automatically — or use the Automations "Apply checklist template" action to stamp them on arrival.
  • Use the search bar when the board gets crowded — type a keyword to instantly filter to matching cards.
  • Bulk-move cards instead of dragging one at a time when you need to push a group of tasks to the next stage at once.
  • Add a cover image to high-priority cards so they visually stand out in the column at a glance.
  • Submit availability early — the sooner your admin has your available times, the better your shifts will fit your schedule.
  • Use shift swaps proactively — submit a swap request as early as possible so your teammate has time to approve before the shift date.
  • Watch for open shift notifications — volunteer slots fill up fast; claim them quickly from the Schedule view.
  • Pin the app to your home screen (PWA install) for fastest access and full-screen experience.
  • Use the QR codes on storage bins to print and label physical bins — teammates can scan to check items out without navigating the app manually.

Common Questions

Can I use Commuter Life on my phone?

Yes — the app is mobile-first. Install it to your home screen for the best experience with a native bottom tab bar and full-screen layout.

Do I need to save my changes manually?

No. Every change you make — task edits, card moves, checklist updates — saves automatically in real time.

Who can add or remove team members?

Only workspace admins can add or remove members. If you need someone added, contact your admin.

How do I stop getting so many notifications?

Go to Settings → Notifications and toggle off the categories you don't need. You can re-enable them at any time.

Can someone outside the team see our board?

No — the board and most views require login. The only public-facing pages are individual storage bin pages (via QR code link) and the worker availability link, if shared by an admin.

What happens if I accidentally delete a task?

Deleted tasks are removed permanently. Before using bulk delete, double-check your selection. If in doubt, move the card to a "Review" or "Archive" column instead of deleting.

What happens when a swap is approved?

The shift is immediately reassigned to the approving teammate on the schedule grid. Both workers receive a notification confirming the change — no admin action required.

How do I get my availability link?

Ask your workspace admin. They generate a unique link from your worker profile in the Schedule view and share it with you directly.

Can I be in more than one workspace?

Yes — the same email account can have access to multiple workspaces. Use the Workspace Switcher (click the workspace name in the header) to jump between them.
